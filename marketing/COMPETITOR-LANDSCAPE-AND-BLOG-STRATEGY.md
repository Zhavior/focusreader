## 1. The one-sentence positioning

Everyone else sells **listening**. Hyperfi sells **finishing**.

Speechify sells "read faster." ElevenLabs sells "beautiful voices." Brain.fm sells
"focus music." Hyperfi is the only product whose feature set attacks the three
things that actually stop an ADHD student: **starting** (time math makes tasks feel
finishable), **staying** (checkpoints + noise beds), and **retaining** (read-along
dual coding). Every blog post should ladder back to *finish*, never *listen*.

## 2. Competitor map

### Direct (TTS readers)

| Competitor | Price | Their strength | Their gap = our angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Speechify** | ~$139/yr | Brand, celebrity voices, word highlighting, huge SEO moat | Built for everyone → optimized for no one. No checkpoints, no time math, no soundscapes. Sells speed, not completion. Aggressive upsells annoy users (read their app-store reviews — content goldmine) |
| **ElevenLabs Reader** | Free/cheap | Best voices on earth, free tier | It's a voice demo, not a study tool. No library workflow, no ADHD features. **The hard question we must answer in content: "why pay when this is free?" Answer: a voice isn't a system for finishing.** |
| **NaturalReader** | Free–$110/yr | Cheap, education market presence | Dated UX, zero focus features. Easy comparison win |
| **Voice Dream Reader** | ~$80/yr | Beloved in accessibility/dyslexia circles, iOS strong | iOS-first, aging, acquired (subscription backlash in community — tread respectfully, their users are loyal) |
| **Audemic** | ~$60/yr | Academic papers niche | Papers only; grad-student niche. Potential ally/adjacent, not enemy |

### Indirect (focus tools — we steal budget from these too)

| Competitor | Why they matter to us |
|---|---|
| **Brain.fm** (~$70/yr) | Sells "science-backed focus music." Our noise beds are *included* — "your focus music and your reading in one subscription" |
| **Endel** | Adaptive soundscapes, big brand deals. Same angle as above |
| **Goblin Tools** | Free, beloved, ADHD task-breakdown. NOT a competitor — a culture reference. Respect it in content; its fans are our exact audience |
| **Forest / Focusmate** | Body-doubling & gamified focus. Useful for "ADHD toolkit" listicles where we appear alongside them |

### The uncomfortable truth to never say out loud but always design around
Our tech is replicable. The moat is **niche love + workflow lock-in** (library,
resume positions, streaks of finished readings). Content's job is to build the
niche love faster than incumbents notice the niche.

## 3. Blog pillars & the first 12 posts

**Pillar A — "Finish your reading" (product-adjacent, converts)**
1. *Why you can't finish reading (and why it's not laziness)* — initiation friction, time blindness, working memory. Validating, science-grounded. Cornerstone post.
2. *I turned my 47-minute reading into 26 minutes — the time math ADHD brains need* — product demo disguised as method.
3. *The checkpoint method: why 5-minute sections beat 2-hour study blocks*
4. *Listening at 2x isn't cheating — the research on TTS + comprehension*

**Pillar B — comparisons (high-intent SEO, converts hardest)**
5. *Speechify vs Hyperfi: which is actually built for ADHD?* (honest — concede their voice catalog, win on completion features)
6. *ElevenLabs Reader is free. Here's when it's enough — and when it isn't* (the brave post; being honest about a free rival earns disproportionate trust)
7. *The 7 best TTS apps for students in 2026* (include everyone honestly; we win by being the only one with an ADHD row in the table)
8. *Brain.fm + Speechify vs one Hyperfi subscription: the math*

**Pillar C — ADHD student life (top of funnel, builds the niche love)**
9. *How to read a textbook chapter when your brain refuses* (mention product once)
10. *Time blindness is why your study plan keeps failing*
11. *Body doubling, brown noise, and other ADHD study hacks that actually have evidence*
12. *"Just use a planner" — and other advice that doesn't survive contact with ADHD*

**Cadence:** 1/week minimum. Pillar C shares best on socials; Pillar B ranks; Pillar A converts. Every post follows WRITER-RULES.md (companion doc).

## 4. Channels & landmines

- **r/ADHD**: bans self-promotion outright. NEVER link-drop. Participate genuinely for months or don't go at all. r/ADHD_Programmers, r/college more tolerant of "I built a thing" posts with full disclosure.
- **TikTok/#adhdtok + #studytok**: the real growth channel. Screen-record the karaoke highlight + checkpoint moments — the product is inherently visual. Duet/stitch ADHD creators; several will trade a demo for a free year.
- **SEO reality check**: Speechify owns the head terms. Target long-tail intent: "how to finish reading with adhd", "text to speech for adhd students", "speechify alternative adhd".
- **Student disability offices / ADHD coaches**: slow, but one newsletter mention converts better than 10k impressions. Offer edu discounts early.

## 5. Proof we need to manufacture (ethically)
Testimonials with completion numbers ("finished all 6 chapters before finals for the
first time"), a public "chapters finished this week" counter once real users exist,
and before/after screen recordings. Collect from the first 10 users in exchange for
free months — get written consent.
